# Plan 4: Minimum Viable Path & Emotional Arc

**Goal:** A student knows exactly what's required vs optional in any month, and Phase 5 authors have a structural design for the final 3 months that prevents final-stretch dropout through peak/valley/peak pacing.

**Purpose:** Two of the highest dropout predictors are pile-up (skipping too many lessons and feeling behind) and final-stretch fatigue (running out of steam at Month 10). The Core/Enrichment split solves pile-up by making 30% of content explicitly optional. The emotional arc design documents solve final-stretch fatigue by making Months 10-12 structurally different.

**Output:** Core vs Enrichment Guide (per-month mapping) + Emotional Arc Guide (peak/valley/peak design spec) + updated Month 10-12 MOCs with structural enforcement.

    ```
    # Core vs Enrichment Guide

    ## Why a Minimum Viable Path?

    Research on self-guided learning shows that **pile-up** (accumulating incomplete exercises
    and unread lessons) is a primary cause of dropout. By Month 04, students who try to do everything
    often feel behind — and stop entirely.

    The solution: **Core vs Enrichment.** Every month defines:
    - **Core** (~70% of effort): The essential lessons covering all 4 threads at this month's
      target depth. Completing Core ensures you meet the month's outcome.
    - **Enrichment** (~30% of effort): Optional deep-dives, field-specific alternatives, and
      advanced exercises. Skipping these does NOT affect your progression.
    ```

    **Section 2: Mechanics**
    - Core lessons marked with `#core` in monthly MOC week tables

    - Progress dashboard (Phase 2) tracks Core completion only

    **Section 3: Skip Strategy** (per CONTEXT.md D-07)
    ```
    ### If You Have Limited Time

    | Time Available | Do This |
    |----------------|---------|
    | 3 hours/week | Core lessons only — skip all enrichment |
    | 4 hours/week | Core lessons + 1 enrichment deep-dive |
    | 5 hours/week | Core + 2 enrichment — full experience |

    ### If You Skip Content

    - **Skipped an Enrichment item?** No action needed. You still meet the month's outcome.
    - **Skipped a Core item?** Add it to your "revisit" list. Revisit before the next month starts.
    - **Missed a whole week?** Don't double up next week. Do the Core items from the missed week
      and skip enrichment for both weeks.
    ```

    ```
    # Emotional Arc Guide: Months 10-12

    ## Why This Matters

    Research on self-directed learning shows that Months 9-12 are the highest-risk dropout window.
    Three compounding factors:
    1. **Isolation fatigue** — 9+ months of solo work with initial novelty long gone
    2. **Pile-up effect** — incomplete exercises and unread lessons accumulated
    3. **Finish-line anxiety** — pressure to "do it right" causes avoidance

    The emotional arc addresses all three by changing **what the student does each week**,
    not just how content is framed.
    ```

    **Section 2: The Arc Shape** (from CONTEXT.md D-08)
    - **Month 10: Peak** — First Complete Paper Audit (exciting, messy, milestone)
    - **Month 11: Valley / Consolidation** — Gap-Filling & Review (lighter, permission to rest)
    - **Month 12: Peak** — Final Research Audit (triumphant, integrated, student's own paper)

    **Section 3: Detailed Design Specifications**

    Create a table per month with structural specifications:

    **Month 10 Design Spec:**
    | Element | Specification | Rationale |
    |---------|---------------|-----------|
    | Lesson length | Short (15-20 min) | Higher throughput = more progress signals |
    | New concepts | Few — mostly integration | Focus is applying, not learning |
    | Exercise volume | High (many small exercises) | Volume creates sense of accomplishment |
    | Autonomy level | Low (structured audit) | First audit needs scaffolding, not freedom |
    | Emotional tone | Exciting, honest about mess | "This will be imperfect — that's the point" |
    | Milestone artifact | First complete paper audit (messy) | Tangible output marks identity shift |
    | Week structure | 4 weeks: 2 prep weeks, 1 audit week, 1 reflect week | Audit week is the milestone |
    | Dear Researcher tone | "You can do this" | Confidence-building, permission to be imperfect |
    | Core/Enrichment | All Core (integration month) | No optional content during milestone |

    **Month 11 Design Spec:**
    | Element | Specification | Rationale |
    |---------|---------------|-----------|
    | Lesson length | Minimal (10 min max) | Genuinely lighter cognitive load |
    | New concepts | None | Only review and re-application |
    | Exercise volume | Low (choice-driven) | Student chooses 2-3 weak areas |
    | Autonomy level | High (choose gaps from menu) | Restores autonomy per self-determination theory |
    | Emotional tone | Softer, permission-giving | "It's OK to rest. This month is designed for it." |
    | Milestone artifact | None | Deliberately low-stakes |
    | Week structure | 2 structured weeks + "Pick your gaps" + journal revisit | 50% fewer required rows |
    | Dear Researcher tone | "It's OK to rest" | Normalizes taking a break |
    | Gap menu | List: Logic review, Probability, Study design, Bias detection, Calibration with pointers to months 01-09 | Choice architecture reduces overwhelm |

    **Month 12 Design Spec:**
    | Element | Specification | Rationale |
    |---------|---------------|-----------|
    | Lesson length | Medium (20-30 min) | Capstone requires sustained focus |
    | New concepts | Integration only | No new theory — framework synthesis |
    | Exercise volume | Medium (guided audit) | One large artifact, not many small exercises |
    | Autonomy level | High (own paper choice) | Intrinsic motivation peak |
    | Emotional tone | Triumphant, forward-looking | "You're a different thinker now" |
    | Milestone artifact | Final Research Audit + Certificate of Completion | Tangible endpoint |
    | Week structure | 1: Choose paper, 2: Run audit, 3: Refine, 4: Celebrate | Clear narrative arc within the month |
    | Dear Researcher tone | "This is the beginning, not the end" | Reframes completion as capability, not termination |
