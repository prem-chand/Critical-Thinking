# Moral Philosophy Reading List

A curated sequence of 12 books on ethics and moral philosophy — compiled from a video reading list (timestamps in parentheses). Together they trace how humans reason about right, wrong, fairness, and obligation: the discipline of thinking clearly about values, which is critical thinking applied to how we ought to live.

## The 12 Books

1. **[[Books/Totality and Infinity|Totality and Infinity]]** — Emmanuel Levinas (0:27–1:45): ethical responsibility to others precedes our own freedom and understanding.
2. **[[Books/The Genealogy of Morals|The Genealogy of Morals]]** — Friedrich Nietzsche (1:46–2:30): challenges the origins of our moral beliefs to loosen ideological rigidity.
3. **[[Books/Reasons and Persons|Reasons and Persons]]** — Derek Parfit (2:31–3:45): personal identity and moral truth, questioning standard rational-choice assumptions.
4. **[[Books/Nicomachean Ethics|Nicomachean Ethics]]** — Aristotle (3:46–4:32): foundational concepts like the *golden mean* and *telos*.
5. **[[Books/Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals|Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals]]** — Immanuel Kant (4:33–5:48): the *categorical imperative* and the consistency of one's values.
6. **[[Books/Utilitarianism|Utilitarianism]]** — John Stuart Mill (5:49–6:29): outcome-based ethics — "the greatest happiness for the greatest number."
7. **[[Books/A Theory of Justice|A Theory of Justice]]** — John Rawls (6:30–7:09): the *veil of ignorance* for reasoning objectively about fairness.
8. **[[Books/What We Owe to Each Other|What We Owe to Each Other]]** — T.M. Scanlon (7:10–7:42): weigh others' reasons with the same seriousness as your own.
9. **[[Books/The Right and the Good|The Right and the Good]]** — W.D. Ross (7:43–8:11): conflicting *prima facie* duties with no single overarching rule.
10. **[[Books/The Normative Web|The Normative Web]]** — Terence Cuneo (8:12–9:21): rejecting moral truth undermines any reason to believe anything at all.
11. **[[Books/From Morality to Virtue|From Morality to Virtue]]** — Michael Slote (9:22–9:55): the "self–other asymmetry" and our neglected duties to ourselves.
12. **[[Books/Principia Ethica|Principia Ethica]]** — G.E. Moore (9:56–10:56): the "open question argument" and precision in defining moral terms.
