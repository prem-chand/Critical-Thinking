# Munger's 25 Tendencies

**Source:** Charlie Munger, *The Psychology of Human Misjudgment* (1995 speech; collected in *Poor Charlie's Almanack*).

Munger's catalog of 25 standard causes of human misjudgment — the psychological tendencies that, singly and especially in combination, drive people to irrational decisions. This page is a hub: tendencies that already have (or now have) their own atomic note are linked; the rest are summarized inline.

> The most important: tendencies rarely act alone. When several push the same way, you get the [[Lollapalooza Tendency|Lollapalooza]] effect (#25).

## The 25 Tendencies

1. **Reward and Punishment Superresponse** — People respond powerfully to incentives, often rationalizing bad conduct to get the reward. → [[Mental Models/Incentives|Incentives]]
2. **[[Liking and Loving Tendency|Liking / Loving]]** — We ignore faults in, and comply with, people and things we like or love.
3. **[[Disliking and Hating Tendency|Disliking / Hating]]** — We ignore virtues in, and distort facts against, what we dislike.
4. **[[Doubt-Avoidance Tendency|Doubt-Avoidance]]** — The mind rushes to remove doubt, especially under puzzlement and stress.
5. **[[Inconsistency-Avoidance Tendency|Inconsistency-Avoidance]]** — We resist changing established conclusions, habits, and commitments.
6. **Curiosity** — A constructive tendency; curiosity counteracts the others and yields wisdom and pleasure.
7. **Kantian Fairness** — We expect and extend the fair-reciprocal behavior that would work if everyone adopted it.
8. **[[Envy and Jealousy Tendency|Envy / Jealousy]]** — Resentment of those with more distorts judgment; rarely admitted openly.
9. **[[Reciprocation Tendency|Reciprocation]]** — We automatically repay favors and disfavors — a lever for manipulation.
10. **[[Influence from Mere Association]]** — We misjudge by association with past pleasure/pain or liked/disliked things (and stereotyping).
11. **Simple, Pain-Avoiding Psychological Denial** — When reality is too painful, we distort it to bear it. → [[Denial]]
12. **Excessive Self-Regard** — We overrate ourselves and our possessions (endowment effect). → [[Overconfidence]]
13. **Overoptimism** — We are excessively optimistic even absent any threat. → [[Overconfidence]], [[Wishful Thinking]]
14. **[[Deprival-Superreaction Tendency|Deprival-Superreaction]]** — Losses and near-misses hurt far more than equivalent gains please (loss aversion).
15. **[[Social Proof]]** — We think and act as those around us do, especially under stress.
16. **[[Contrast Misreaction Tendency|Contrast-Misreaction]]** — We judge by contrast with what came before, not by absolute value.
17. **Stress-Influence** — Light stress sharpens performance; heavy stress causes dysfunction and acute pessimism.
18. **Availability-Misweighing** — We overweigh vivid, easily recalled information. → [[Availability Bias]]
19. **Use-It-or-Lose-It** — Skills deteriorate with disuse; practice slows the loss and speeds recovery.
20. **Drug-Misinfluence** — Chemical dependence destroys cognition and judgment (an extension of denial, #11).
21. **Senescence-Misinfluence** — Cognitive decay with age; continual joyful learning delays but cannot prevent it.
22. **[[Authority-Misinfluence Tendency|Authority-Misinfluence]]** — We defer to leaders automatically, even when they are wrong.
23. **Twaddle** — Gifted talkers produce volumes of meaningless chatter that crowds out serious work.
24. **Reason-Respecting** — We comply and learn far better when given reasons — so much so that even bad reasons boost compliance.
25. **[[Lollapalooza Tendency|Lollapalooza]]** — Extreme outcomes when several tendencies combine and reinforce one another in the same direction.

## How to Use This
- Munger's prescription: build the tendencies into a mental **checklist** and run decisions against it, looking especially for *stacked* tendencies (#25).
- Several of these map onto biases already in the vault — see the links above and the [[Cognitive Biases]] MOC.
