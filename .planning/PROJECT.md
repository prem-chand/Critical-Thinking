# Critical Thinking for Researchers — 12-Month Curriculum

## What This Is

A 12-month structured curriculum that teaches researchers critical thinking through an integrated lens of philosophy, logic, probability, statistics, scientific reasoning, and paper critique. The entire curriculum lives as a single Obsidian knowledge graph — a web of interlinked notes, exercises, templates, and structured claims that students build and inhabit throughout the year. Built atop the existing Critical Thinking vault, extending its Biases, Fallacies, Mental Models, and Claim Analysis sections with progressive learning materials.

## Core Value

Transform a researcher's relationship with their own thinking — from passive consumer of papers and producer of analyses to a self-calibrating reasoner who explicitly tracks beliefs, quantifies uncertainty, and catches their own cognitive errors before they become published mistakes.

## Context

- **Existing foundation:** This vault already contains notes on cognitive biases, logical fallacies, mental models, book notes, structured claim analyses, forecasts, and paper reviews. These serve as the reference library that the curriculum layers structured learning paths on top of.
- **Audience:** Researchers (graduate students, postdocs, early-career scientists) who want systematic training in critical thinking beyond the "be skeptical" advice typical in lab culture.
- **Format:** Obsidian vault. Every month has a dedicated MOC (Map of Content) with daily/weekly notes, linked exercises, and progressive templates. Students interact with the same knowledge graph they're building.
- **Philosophical spine:** The curriculum treats critical thinking as a skill (not a disposition), drawing on Bayesian epistemology, falsificationism, and the replication crisis as its core case studies.
- **Guiding principle:** Don't teach theory in isolation — immediately apply every concept to real papers in the student's field.

### Out of Scope

- Creating full-length video lectures or narrated content — text-first, Obsidian-native only
- Building custom Obsidian plugins — leverage existing community plugins
- Individualized tutoring or feedback — curriculum is self-guided
- Domain-specific statistics (bioinformatics, econometrics, etc.) — teach general principles with domain-agnostic examples

## Constraints

- **Platform:** Obsidian-only. No external tooling, web apps, or databases.
- **Format:** Markdown notes with YAML frontmatter, wikilinks, and Obsidian-flavored syntax.
- **Duration:** Must be completable in 12 months at 3-5 hours/week.
- **Existing vault:** Must not break or restructure the existing vault — curriculum content extends it in a self-contained subdirectory.
- **Plugins:** Dataview and Charts are available for dashboards; no assumption beyond core plugins.
