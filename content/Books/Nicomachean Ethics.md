# Nicomachean Ethics

**Nicomachean Ethics** (c. 350 BCE) by **Aristotle** is a foundational work of Western ethics, asking what the good life (*eudaimonia*) is and how virtuous character is formed.

## Core Idea
Virtue is a *hexis* (settled disposition) cultivated through habit, and each virtue lies as a "golden mean" between extremes of excess and deficiency — courage between recklessness and cowardice. Human action aims at a *telos* (end/purpose), and the highest end is flourishing achieved through reason and practical wisdom (*phronesis*).

## Why It Matters for Critical Thinking
Introduces *phronesis* — practical wisdom in judging particular situations — and the idea that good judgment is trained, not innate. The golden mean and telos remain durable lenses for evaluating goals and conduct.
