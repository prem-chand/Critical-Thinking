# Emotional Arc Guide — Peak / Valley / Peak

## Why Emotional Pacing Matters

Self-guided curricula suffer from **final-stretch dropout** (see [[.planning/research/PITFALLS.md|PITFALLS.md]], P16): students who sustain 9 months of effort disengage just before the finish because Months 10-12 feel like "more of the same." The solution is a deliberate **peak/valley/peak** emotional arc that varies pacing, stakes, and cognitive load to maintain momentum.

- **Peak:** High-stakes milestone with a tangible output. Celebrated explicitly.
- **Valley:** Low-stakes consolidation. Permission to rest, review, and fill gaps.
- **Peak:** Capstone culmination. Reduced content load to free mental space for synthesis.

## Month 10 — Peak: "First Complete Paper Audit"

- **Duration:** 4 weeks
- **Framing:** Messy-by-design. The goal is *experiencing* the full audit workflow, not perfection.
- **Content load:** Full (10 lessons, all core)
- **Output:** First messy draft of a Research Audit Framework evaluation
- **Celebration milestone:** Explicit marker in MOC, callout box congratulating the student

## Month 11 — Valley: "Gap-Filling & Review"

- **Duration:** 3 weeks
- **Framing:** The valley is intentional — research on self-guided learning shows that sustained high intensity leads to dropout (PITFALLS.md P16, P11). Students are given explicit permission to slow down.
- **Content load:** Reduced (~50% of normal: 4 core + 2 enrichment)
- **Structure:** Gap Menu where students select 2-3 weak areas from their Month 10 audit
- **Tone:** Restorative, not pushy. "You've done the hard part. Now fill the cracks."

## Month 12 — Peak: "Final Research Audit" Capstone

- **Duration:** 3 weeks
- **Framing:** The capstone — reduced intro content leaves room for the final audit itself.
- **Content load:** Light (6 lessons, all core)
- **Output:** Polished, reusable RAF evaluation of a paper of the student's choice
- **Celebration:** Certificate of Completion template, final calibration summary

## Structural Diff: M10 vs M11 vs M12

| Dimension | Month 10 (Peak) | Month 11 (Valley) | Month 12 (Peak) |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Weeks | 4 | 3 | 3 |
| Lessons | 10 | 6 | 6 |
| Core | 10 | 4 | 6 |
| Enrichment | 0 | 2 | 0 |
| Student choice | Low | High (Gap Menu) | High (paper choice) |
| Output | Messy audit | Gap review notes | Polished audit + certificate |
| Cognitive load | High | Low | Moderate |
| Tone | "Go for it, it's OK to be messy" | "Rest and review" | "Bring it home" |

## Research Basis

P16 in PITFALLS.md documents the "final-stretch dropout" pattern from SheTalks (2026) and multiple MOOC dropout reviews. The peak/valley/peak design directly counters this by varying intensity, providing explicit milestone celebrations, and giving students permission to slow down before the finish line. Without this arc, Months 10-12 would read as "more of the same" — the exact structural failure that causes ~50% of late-curriculum attrition.
