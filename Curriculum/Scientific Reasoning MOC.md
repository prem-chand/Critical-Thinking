# Scientific Reasoning

Scientific reasoning is critical thinking applied to empirical inquiry — understanding how science works, how studies are designed, what can go wrong, and how to evaluate evidence systematically. This thread builds from philosophy of science through study design to full replication auditing.

## Spiral Progression

| Phase | Months | Depth | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|-------|
| Foundation | 1—3 | Level 1 | What is science, falsification, hypothesis testing basics |
| Application | 4—6 | Level 2 | Study design, confounds, sampling, measurement validity |
| Synthesis | 7—9 | Level 3 | Causal inference, replication crisis, bias taxonomies |
| Teaching | 10—12 | Level 4 | Complete paper audit, replication audit, peer review |
