# First Principles Thinking

Breaking down a problem into its most fundamental truths and reasoning up from there — rather than reasoning by analogy or convention.

## The Idea
Instead of accepting existing solutions as given, strip away assumptions until you reach the irreducible facts. Then rebuild your understanding from the ground up.

## Examples
- Elon Musk on rocket costs: instead of accepting the price of rockets, he calculated the raw material cost and realized rockets could be built for ~2% of the market price
- Aristotle: "the first basis from which a thing is known"
- Physics: deriving complex phenomena from fundamental laws rather than memorizing formulas

## How to Apply
1. Identify the problem or belief
2. Ask "What do I know to be absolutely true about this?"
3. Challenge every assumption — especially the invisible ones
4. Reconstruct from base truths

## Key Questions
- "What are we absolutely sure is true?"
- "What assumptions am I making that I haven't examined?"
- "If I started from scratch today, would I do it this way?"
