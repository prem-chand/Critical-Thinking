# 12-Month Outcomes

All outcomes follow the format *"After Month N, the student can [verb] [object] by [method]."* The progression follows Bloom's Taxonomy from Remember (M01) through Create (M12).

## Month 01 — Foundations (Remember)

After Month 01, the student can **recall** the core definitions of critical thinking, truth, belief, and knowledge by **explaining** the difference between factual claims, value claims, and interpretive claims in a research context.

[[Curriculum/Month 01/Month 01 MOC|Month 01 MOC]]

## Month 02 — Logic & Argumentation (Understand)

After Month 02, the student can **classify** argument structures as deductive, inductive, or abductive by **identifying** premise-conclusion patterns in published research abstracts.

[[Curriculum/Month 02/Month 02 MOC|Month 02 MOC]]

## Month 03 — Bias & Heuristics (Apply)

After Month 03, the student can **demonstrate** the effect of at least five cognitive biases on research interpretation by **modifying** their reading workflow to include bias-checking steps.

[[Curriculum/Month 03/Month 03 MOC|Month 03 MOC]]

## Month 04 — Probability & Bayesian Thinking (Apply)

After Month 04, the student can **compute** a posterior probability from prior and likelihood using Bayes' theorem by **applying** Bayesian updating to a published claim with explicit priors.

[[Curriculum/Month 04/Month 04 MOC|Month 04 MOC]]

## Month 05 — Statistical Reasoning (Analyze)

After Month 05, the student can **differentiate** between statistical significance, practical significance, and replication credibility by **interrogating** p-values, effect sizes, and confidence intervals in a published study.

[[Curriculum/Month 05/Month 05 MOC|Month 05 MOC]]

## Month 06 — Causal Reasoning (Analyze)

After Month 06, the student can **distinguish** causal from associational claims by **constructing** a directed acyclic graph (DAG) that identifies confounders, colliders, and mediators in a given research design.

[[Curriculum/Month 06/Month 06 MOC|Month 06 MOC]]

## Month 07 — Scientific Methodology (Analyze)

After Month 07, the student can **critique** a study's methodological choices against the norms of falsification, reproducibility, and research ethics by **auditing** sample size justification, pre-registration status, and conflict-of-interest disclosures.

[[Curriculum/Month 07/Month 07 MOC|Month 07 MOC]]

## Month 08 — Argument Mapping & Critical Writing (Evaluate)

After Month 08, the student can **evaluate** the strength of an argument chain in a research paper by **diagramming** it as an argument map with explicit premises, hidden assumptions, and conclusions.

[[Curriculum/Month 08/Month 08 MOC|Month 08 MOC]]

## Month 09 — Forecasting & Calibration (Evaluate)

After Month 09, the student can **assess** their own calibration accuracy by **generating** probabilistic forecasts with Brier-score feedback over a sustained tracking period.

[[Curriculum/Month 09/Month 09 MOC|Month 09 MOC]]

## Month 10 — Integration & Synthesis (Evaluate)

After Month 10, the student can **appraise** a complete research paper across all six disciplinary lenses by **producing** a structured paper critique that integrates logic, probability, statistics, and scientific reasoning checks.

[[Curriculum/Month 10/Month 10 MOC|Month 10 MOC]]

## Month 11 — Advanced Applications (Create)

After Month 11, the student can **construct** a peer-review-quality evaluation of a pre-print by **synthesizing** bias checks, methodology audit, statistical critique, Bayesian belief update, and argument mapping into a single coherent document.

[[Curriculum/Month 11/Month 11 MOC|Month 11 MOC]]

## Month 12 — Synthesis & Mastery (Create)

After Month 12, the student can **produce** an independent audit of any research paper in their field by **integrating** all six disciplines into a calibrated, probabilistic, metacognitive assessment that identifies their own residual uncertainties.

[[Curriculum/Month 12/Month 12 MOC|Month 12 MOC]]

## Bloom's Taxonomy Progression

| Months | Dominant Level | What Changes |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| M01–M03 | Remember → Understand → Apply | Foundational vocabulary and recognition |
| M04–M06 | Apply → Analyze | Active tool use, decomposition of claims |
| M07–M09 | Analyze → Evaluate | Methodological critique, self-assessment |
| M10–M12 | Evaluate → Create | Synthesis, independent production |
