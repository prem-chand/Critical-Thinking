# Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals

**Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals** (1785) by **Immanuel Kant** seeks the supreme principle of morality and locates it in reason itself: the *categorical imperative*.

## Core Idea
Kant argues that moral worth comes from acting out of duty according to principles (*maxims*) one could will to be universal laws. The categorical imperative's formulations — universalizability, and treating humanity always as an end and never merely as a means — make consistency the test of a moral rule.

## Why It Matters for Critical Thinking
The universalizability test is a powerful consistency check: "What if everyone reasoned this way?" forces you to examine whether your values survive being applied without special exceptions for yourself.
